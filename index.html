Trick or Treat!

by: Rachel Yang

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I made this game to go with the holiday that happened during the creation of my game which was Halloween. I planned to make the original spaceship or moving object into a "trick or treating" ghost and then have the aliens become stationary and transform them into candy for the ghost to collect, therefore, it reflects the holiday of Halloween.

2. What I Learned

I learned from user testing that everything can always be improved. Feedback I received from teachers and classmates that led to changes in my game were to add a timer and a counter for the amount of candy collected, to improve my collision boxes, and to make the movement of the ghost smoother.

3. Architecture of My Game

The different parts of my game are: the main object (ghost), and the secondary objects (candy). The objective or goal of this game is to collect all 100 pieces in the least time possible. You move the ghost using arrow keys which match the direction it will move on screen. Then, try to "collect" or overlap the ghost over the candy. When the candy's collision box meets the ghost's collision box, the candy will disappear, supposedly into the candy basket and you will earn a point.

4. Technical Implementation

Because my game setup was similar to League Invaders, I was able to adjust and add/subtract code to make a new and different game. Firstly, the original aliens that moved down and were supposed to be removed by a projectile were turned into unmoving candy. Also, the projectile class was removed and not replaced by something else. Finally, when the ghost hits the candy, the candy is eliminated instead of the ghost as it was in League Invaders when the game ended when the spaceship was hit by an alien. A particular aspect of the game I am proud of is that I looked at the details of my game and made sure they were as good as the main parts of the game. An example of this was making sure candy did not spawn to block the timer and counter text on screen. Also, polishing the movement of the ghost was something unnecessary, but could be better. These factors all contribute to the overall game.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of this project was to fix all the big problems that appeared in my game late into the process of making it. One was the collision box of the ghost. At one point, when the ghost moved, it would collect candy way above or below it. The problem was that the ghost's collision box was not centered on the ghost, rather slightly to the right and top of it. Another difficulty was implementing the changes recommended to me after user testing. After some help, I managed to fix my game and complete it.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

Some improvements I would make in the future if I were to continue this game are getting a better background image, making different values for different types of candies, and adding challenges to make the game more interesting and longer.
